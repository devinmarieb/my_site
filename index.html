We can start at the very beginning. I had very typical high school jobs. I worked in both a movie theater and a AAA baseball stadium, Hadlock Field, home of the Portland Seadogs, formally owned by the Marlins, now owned by the Red Sox (if you're into that kind of thing). The switch from teal to navy blue did not go over well with some people in the beginning, and to this day, teal merchandise is coveted.

I also held two very relatable jobs in college - a retail worker at a clothing store and an assistant manager at a candy store. I got my first cavity after that year, but I also learned I love black licorice and marzipan.

After college, the majority of my young adult life was spent going through the ranks at an Apple retail store. I was a Specialist, a term used for the majority of people you interact with at the stores, a Lead Cashier, a Visual Merchandising Lead (yes I got to touch all the new products days in advance), and finally a Scheduling Administrator for employee schedules. Aside from having good times and making great friends, I also learned I never want to work in customer service at that level ever again. I don't know if you've ever seen an iPhone launch in a major city, or what it looks like when one half of a couple realizes that when you share an iCloud account all your photos are accessible to each other, but neither situation is pretty.

After my quarter-life crisis and a career change to development, I moved from elevation to below sea level to do creative frontend work with WeTransfer. The majority of my work revolves around a variety of interactive and animated client advertising projects. Beyond that, I've built mini sites like Craig Taylor: Anything but guns and Ideas Report 2018. I also manage and maintain Ideas by WeTransfer, a creative blog, and have created custom articles for our content blog, WePresent. For a little while, I also made custom email templates for our marketing and brand emails.

I really like SVGs.

Is the design of this site purposfully kind of messy? Yes. I use this site as a creative outlet, so it changes occassionally. Right now I'm feeling kind of messy. Anyway, I'm an only child who is also a Taurus, which is another way of saying I'm incredibly independent and stubborn.

Yes, I'm into true crime, I have been since childhood. This might shock a lot of people, but if you asked me if I wanted to listen to music or a podcast, I'd respond with "podcast" in almost all circumstances. Though, just so you can judge my personal tastes, my favorite song of all time is Fleetwood Mac's 1976 live performance of "Rhiannon."
